# GDPR Breach Notification Process

**Regulation:** GDPR Art. 33, 34  
**Owner:** Data Protection Officer (DPO)

---

## 1. Detection & Assessment

1. **Detect** potential breach (unauthorized access, loss, alteration of personal data).
2. **Assess** within 24–48 hours:
   - Scope: How many individuals affected?
   - Data types: PII, PHI, financial?
   - Risk to rights and freedoms: High / Medium / Low?

---

## 2. Notify Supervisory Authority (DPA)

- **When:** Without undue delay, **within 72 hours** of becoming aware.
- **If >72h:** Explain the delay.
- **Content:** Nature of breach, categories/approx. number of data subjects, likely consequences, measures taken.
- **Channel:** DPA online portal or designated contact.

---

## 3. Notify Data Subjects

- **When:** If high risk to rights and freedoms.
- **Without undue delay.**
- **Content:** Nature of breach, contact point (DPO), likely consequences, measures taken.

**Template (adapt as needed):**

```
Subject: Important Security Notice – [Product Name]

Dear [User],

We are writing to inform you of a security incident that may have affected your personal data.

What happened: [Brief description]
What information was involved: [Data categories]
What we are doing: [Remediation steps]
What you can do: [Recommendations, e.g. change password]
Contact: [DPO email / support link]

We apologize for any concern this may cause.
[Company Name]
```

---

## 4. Document

- Maintain internal record of all breaches (even those not reported).
- Include: facts, effects, remedial action.
